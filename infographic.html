What’s the Problem?

The state (Minas Gerais, Brazil, responsible  for half of the beans   in Brazil) has at least 119,000 coffee plantations and hundreds of thousands of workers but only 245 inspectors.
More than 300 coffee workers were found by   officials in slave-like conditions nationwide in 2018.

The International Women’s Coffee Alliance

The International Women’s Coffee Alliance (IWCA) which is a global network lead by self-driven, local chapters that aim to focus on achieving and learning about sustainable livelihood in their respective communities. It also focuses on the participation of woman in all aspects of the coffee industry.
